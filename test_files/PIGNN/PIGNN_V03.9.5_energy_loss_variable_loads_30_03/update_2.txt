uz has 13 orders of magnitude less gradient than ux. It is literally invisible to the optimizer. This is not a bug — it's a consequence of Fz=0 combined with uz_c being tiny.

Why ux Plateaus at 22%
Even for ux, the energy loss has a conditioning problem. The energy weights displacement errors by their stiffness:

Stiff modes (high eigenvalue of K): large energy contribution → learned first
Soft modes (low eigenvalue): tiny energy contribution → negligible gradient
At 22% error, the model has captured all high-energy modes. The remaining 22% is in soft modes where:

text

∂Π/∂(soft mode amplitude) ≈ λ_soft × amplitude
With λ_soft/λ_stiff = 1/22,000,000, these modes contribute ~0.000005% to the energy. The optimizer sees effectively zero gradient for them.

Should You Add Fz Loads? YES — This Is Critical
Adding Fz loads fixes the problem at its root:

1. Direct gradient signal for uz:

text

∂W/∂pred_uz = Fz × uz_c / E_c ≈ Fz/F_c × (uz_c/ux_c) ≈ 0.027 × Fz/F_c
This is 10 orders of magnitude larger than the current bending-only gradient.

2. uz_c will increase dramatically. With vertical forces, vertical displacements become much larger. If Fz ~ O(10) like Fx:

text

uz_c_new ≈ F_c × L³/(12EI) = 10 × 27/(12×1e-5) ≈ huge (beam theory)
Actually this will be limited by the frame connectivity, but uz_c will grow significantly.

3. Better conditioning. The energy landscape becomes more isotropic — all DOFs contribute meaningfully to both strain energy and work.

In Fact, Mixed Training Is BETTER for Generalization
Training data	What model learns	Generalization
Fx only	Only horizontal force→displacement patterns	❌ Cannot predict Fz response
Fz only	Only vertical force→displacement patterns	❌ Cannot predict Fx response
Fx + Fz mixed	Full K⁻¹ mapping	✅ Any combination works

This way you explicitly verify that the model handles single-component loading correctly.

Bottom Line
Training with richer loads (Fx+Fz) gives the model more information about the structure's behavior, not less. A model that correctly minimizes Π for diverse Fx+Fz combinations has effectively learned K⁻¹, which works for any load combination within the training range.
